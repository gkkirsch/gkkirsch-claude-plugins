## 1. Content Pillar Strategy

Before planning individual pieces, define your content pillars вҖ” the 3-5 core topics that represent your expertise and map to your business goals.

### How to Choose Content Pillars

**Step 1**: List every topic your business could credibly write about (brainstorm 20-30)

**Step 2**: Filter through these three lenses:

| Lens | Question | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Audience Need** | Do your target customers actively search for this? | "email marketing automation" вҶ’ Yes, high search volume |
| **Business Alignment** | Does this topic move people toward your product/service? | "email automation" вҶ’ Yes, our product does this |
| **Competitive Edge** | Can you write about this better than competitors? | Do you have unique data, experience, or perspective? |

A topic must pass ALL THREE lenses to be a pillar.

**Step 3**: Select 3-5 pillars that cover:
- 1-2 **awareness topics** (broad, high-volume, educational)
- 1-2 **consideration topics** (comparison, evaluation, use-case specific)
- 1 **decision topic** (product-adjacent, bottom-funnel)

### Cluster Page Guidelines

| Attribute | Pillar Page | Cluster Page |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Length** | 2,500-5,000 words | 1,500-2,500 words |
| **Keyword** | Head term (high volume, competitive) | Long-tail (lower volume, less competitive) |
| **Depth** | Broad overview of entire topic | Deep dive on specific subtopic |
| **Links** | Links to ALL cluster pages | Links back to pillar + 2-3 other cluster pages |
| **Update Frequency** | Quarterly | As needed |
| **Purpose** | Rank for competitive term, be the hub | Rank for specific queries, feed authority to pillar |

### Internal Linking Rules for Clusters

1. **Pillar вҶ’ Cluster**: The pillar page must link to every cluster page with keyword-rich anchor text
2. **Cluster вҶ’ Pillar**: Every cluster page links back to the pillar page (usually in intro or conclusion)
3. **Cluster вҶ” Cluster**: Cluster pages link to 2-3 related cluster pages within the same topic
4. **Cross-Pillar**: Occasionally link between pillars when genuinely relevant (don't force it)
5. **Anchor Text**: Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text (not "click here" or "read more")

## 4. Publishing Cadence Recommendations

### By Business Size and Resources

| Resources | Blog Posts | Newsletters | Social Posts | Video |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Solo creator** | 1/week | 1/week | 3-5/week | 1/month |
| **Small team (2-3)** | 2-3/week | 1-2/week | 5-7/week | 2-4/month |
| **Content team (4-8)** | 4-5/week | 2-3/week | Daily | Weekly |
| **Enterprise** | Daily | Multiple segments | Multiple daily | 2-3/week |

### Cadence Rules

1. **Consistency beats frequency** вҖ” 1 great post/week for 52 weeks > 5 posts/week for 3 months then burnout
2. **Quality over quantity** вҖ” One 2,500-word comprehensive guide > five 500-word thin posts
3. **Newsletter cadence must be predictable** вҖ” If you say "every Tuesday", it arrives every Tuesday
4. **Social is about presence** вҖ” Daily touchpoints build familiarity (short posts count)
5. **Batch production** вҖ” Write in batches (e.g., 4 blog posts in 2 days) then schedule over the month
6. **Buffer stock** вҖ” Maintain 2-4 weeks of pre-written content as a safety net

### Optimal Publishing Times (Starting Points вҖ” Test Your Own)

| Platform | Best Days | Best Times | Why |
|----------|-----------|------------|-----|
| **Blog** | Tuesday-Thursday | Early morning (publish for SEO indexing) | Weekday traffic peaks |
| **Newsletter** | Tuesday or Thursday | 6-8am or 10am recipient's timezone | High open rates pre-work or mid-morning |
| **LinkedIn** | Tuesday-Thursday | 7-9am, 12pm, 5-6pm | Professional browsing windows |
| **Twitter/X** | Monday-Friday | 8-10am, 12-1pm | Commute + lunch engagement |
| **Instagram** | Monday-Saturday | 11am-1pm, 7-9pm | Lunch + evening browsing |
| **YouTube** | Thursday-Saturday | 2-4pm (publish for weekend viewing) | Leisure viewing patterns |

## 5. Content Audit Framework

### When to Audit

- **Full audit**: Every 6 months or when traffic significantly changes
- **Quick audit**: Monthly вҖ” check top 20 posts, recent publications
- **Triggered audit**: After algorithm updates, site redesign, or strategy shift

### The 4-Bucket Content Audit

Go through every existing content page and place it in one of four buckets:

#### Bucket 1: Keep & Optimize (Pages 4-10 on Google)

These pages are almost there. Small optimizations can push them to page 1.

**Actions**:
- Update title tag and meta description
- Add missing sections (check what competitors cover)
- Improve internal linking (add 2-3 links from authoritative pages)
- Update statistics, examples, and screenshots
- Add FAQ section targeting People Also Ask questions
- Improve readability (shorter paragraphs, more headers, add visuals)

**How to identify**: Search Console вҶ’ Pages with impressions but low CTR or position 4-20

#### Bucket 2: Consolidate (Overlapping / Cannibalizing Pages)

Multiple pages targeting the same keyword dilute your authority.

**Actions**:
- Identify pages targeting the same keyword (Search Console вҶ’ compare pages for same query)
- Choose the stronger page as the winner
- Merge unique content from weaker pages into the winner
- 301 redirect weaker pages to the winner
- Update internal links to point to the winner

**How to identify**: Search Console вҶ’ filter by query, check if multiple URLs appear for the same keyword

#### Bucket 3: Update (Outdated but Valuable)

Content that once performed but has aged out. The topic is still relevant, but the content is stale.

**Actions**:
- Update the year in title and throughout content
- Replace outdated statistics (anything > 2 years old)
- Update screenshots and tool references
- Add new sections covering recent developments
- Refresh examples and case studies
- Update `dateModified` in schema markup
- Republish with current date (some CMS platforms support this)
- Re-promote on social media and newsletter

**How to identify**: Google Analytics вҶ’ pages with declining traffic over 6+ months that still have relevant topics

#### Bucket 4: Prune (Low-Quality or Irrelevant)

Content that doesn't serve any purpose: no traffic, no backlinks, off-topic, thin, or low-quality.

**Actions**:
- If it has any backlinks вҶ’ 301 redirect to the most relevant page
- If it has zero backlinks and zero traffic вҶ’ return 410 (gone) status or noindex
- Don't just delete without redirecting вҖ” broken URLs create 404 errors
- Remove from sitemap

**How to identify**: Google Analytics вҶ’ pages with <10 pageviews in last 12 months AND no backlinks (check Ahrefs/Moz)

## 7. Seasonal & Evergreen Content Balance

### The 80/20 Rule

Target **80% evergreen content** (ranks and drives traffic for years) and **20% timely content** (news, trends, seasonal, event-driven).

### Evergreen Content Characteristics

- Topic is always relevant ("how to write a resume", "email marketing tips")
- Doesn't reference specific dates or events
- Can be updated annually to stay current
- Continues to rank and drive traffic long after publication
- Best for SEO and compounding returns

### Timely Content Characteristics

- References current events, trends, or news
- Has a built-in expiration date
- Drives short-term traffic spikes
- Best for social sharing, newsletters, and brand awareness
- Should be clearly dated

### Seasonal Content Calendar

Plan seasonal content 2-3 months before the season. SEO takes time вҖ” publish seasonal content early.

## 8. Content Workflow & Production Process

### The Content Production Pipeline

```
IDEATION вҶ’ BRIEFING вҶ’ DRAFTING вҶ’ EDITING вҶ’ SEO CHECK вҶ’ DESIGN вҶ’ PUBLISH вҶ’ DISTRIBUTE вҶ’ MEASURE
```

### Stage-by-Stage Process

**1. Ideation** (Monthly вҖ” batch process)
- Review keyword research for opportunities
- Check competitor content for gaps
- Mine customer questions (support, sales, social)
- Review industry news and trends
- Prioritize by business impact and search opportunity

**2. Briefing** (Before every piece)
Content briefs prevent rewrites. Include:
```
## Content Brief: [Working Title]

Target Keyword: [primary keyword]
Secondary Keywords: [2-5 related terms]
Search Intent: [informational/commercial/transactional]
Content Type: [blog/guide/comparison/case study]
Target Word Count: [X words]
Target Audience: [who and what stage of awareness]
Business Goal: [traffic, leads, sales, brand]
Competing Content: [3-5 URLs to outperform]
Key Points to Cover: [must-include topics]
Internal Links: [existing content to link to]
CTA: [what action we want readers to take]
Deadline: [draft due date]
```

**3. Drafting** (Writer creates first draft)
- Follow the content brief
- Write the first draft without self-editing (get ideas down)
- Target 110% of word count (editing always cuts)
- Include placeholder notes for data points, examples, screenshots

**4. Editing** (Editor reviews and refines)
- Structural edit: Is the flow logical? Are sections in the right order?
- Content edit: Are claims supported? Is the advice actionable?
- Copy edit: Grammar, spelling, style consistency
- SEO edit: Keywords placed naturally? Headers optimized?
- Read aloud test: Does it sound natural?

**5. SEO Check** (Before publication)
- Title tag optimized (50-60 chars, keyword front-loaded)
- Meta description written (150-160 chars)
- URL slug set (short, keyword-rich)
- Headers include keyword variations
- Internal links added (3-5)
- External links to authoritative sources (2-3)
- Image alt text written
- Schema markup recommended

**6. Design** (Visual elements)
- Featured image / hero image
- In-content images, diagrams, screenshots
- Infographics or custom visuals
- Social sharing images (OG image)

**7. Publish** (Go live)
- Final proofread
- Check all links work
- Verify formatting on mobile
- Submit URL to Google Search Console for indexing
- Add to XML sitemap

**8. Distribute** (Active promotion)
- Share on all relevant social platforms
- Include in next newsletter
- Send to relevant internal teams
- Outreach to people/companies mentioned in the content
- Submit to relevant communities (if appropriate and not spammy)

**9. Measure** (30/60/90 day review)
- Organic traffic trend
- Keyword rankings movement
- Engagement metrics (time on page, scroll depth, bounce rate)
- Conversion metrics (if applicable)
- Backlinks acquired
- Social shares

## 9. Content Performance Metrics

### What to Track and Why

| Metric | Measures | Tool | Frequency |
|--------|----------|------|-----------|
| **Organic Traffic** | SEO effectiveness | Google Analytics | Weekly |
| **Keyword Rankings** | Search visibility | Search Console, Ahrefs | Weekly |
| **Time on Page** | Content engagement | Google Analytics | Monthly |
| **Scroll Depth** | Content consumption | GA4 events | Monthly |
| **Bounce Rate** | Content relevance | Google Analytics | Monthly |
| **Backlinks** | Content authority | Ahrefs, Moz | Monthly |
| **Social Shares** | Content resonance | BuzzSumo, native analytics | Per post |
| **Email Signups** | Lead generation | Email platform | Per post |
| **Conversion Rate** | Business impact | Google Analytics | Monthly |
| **Revenue Attribution** | ROI | CRM + Analytics | Quarterly |

### Content Scoring Model

Score each piece of content on a 0-100 scale:

```
Content Score = (Traffic Г— 0.25) + (Rankings Г— 0.20) + (Engagement Г— 0.20) + (Conversions Г— 0.20) + (Backlinks Г— 0.15)

Where each component is normalized to 0-100:
- Traffic: vs. site average pageviews
- Rankings: average position for target keywords (position 1 = 100, position 50 = 0)
- Engagement: time on page vs. site average
- Conversions: conversion rate vs. site average
- Backlinks: referring domains vs. site average
```

Use content scores to identify:
- Top performers to replicate and update
- Underperformers to optimize or prune
- Content types that consistently perform well
- Topics that resonate with your audience
