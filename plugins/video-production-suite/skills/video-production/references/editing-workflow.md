# Editing Workflow Reference

Complete editing workflows for each major video editing tool, including keyboard shortcuts, export settings, subtitle handling, and color grading fundamentals for product videos.

---

## Descript — Text-Based Video Editing

Descript's core innovation is that you edit video by editing a text transcript. Delete a word from the text and the corresponding video/audio is removed. This makes it the fastest editor for narration-heavy content.

### When to Use Descript
- Videos driven by voiceover or talking head
- Content where you need to remove filler words ("um", "uh", "like")
- Quick turnaround projects where speed matters more than pixel-perfect control
- Team collaboration where non-editors need to review and comment
- Projects requiring transcription, captions, or translated subtitles

### Descript Editing Workflow

**Step 1: Import and Transcribe**
1. Create a new project and import your video/audio files
2. Descript automatically transcribes the audio (takes 1-3 minutes per hour of footage)
3. Review the transcript for accuracy — correct any misheard words
4. Label speakers if multiple people are talking

**Step 2: Script-Based Editing**
1. Read through the transcript and delete unwanted sections by selecting and deleting text
2. Use "Remove Filler Words" (Edit > Remove Filler Words) to auto-delete "um", "uh", "you know", etc.
3. Rearrange sections by cutting and pasting text blocks — the video follows
4. Use the "Shorten Word Gaps" feature to tighten pacing automatically
5. For multi-take recordings, select the best take of each section in the transcript

**Step 3: Visual Enhancements**
1. Add titles and text overlays from the toolbar
2. Insert screen recordings or B-roll by dragging media into the timeline
3. Use "Scenes" to organize your video into logical sections
4. Add transitions between scenes (dissolve, fade, slide)
5. Apply templates for consistent branding across videos

**Step 4: AI Features**
1. **AI Voice (Overdub)**: Clone your voice and generate new narration by typing text
2. **Studio Sound**: One-click audio cleanup — removes background noise, enhances voice
3. **Eye Contact**: AI-powered eye contact correction for talking head footage
4. **Green Screen**: Remove and replace backgrounds without a physical green screen
5. **Regenerate**: Replace a word or phrase in the narration with AI-generated audio matching your voice

**Step 5: Subtitles and Captions**
1. Go to Captions (toolbar) and click "Generate Captions"
2. Choose style: Descript offers preset caption styles including animated word-by-word highlights
3. Customize font, size, color, position, and background
4. Review and correct any timing issues
5. Export captions as SRT, VTT, or burned into the video

**Step 6: Export**
1. File > Export
2. Choose quality preset or customize:
   - Resolution: 1080p or 4K
   - Quality: High (recommended) or Custom bitrate
   - Format: MP4 (H.264)
3. Select whether to include captions burned in or as sidecar file
4. Export audio-only (MP3/WAV) if needed for podcast repurposing

## CapCut — Fast Social Media Video Editing

CapCut (by ByteDance) excels at quick, trendy social media content with built-in auto-captions, effects, and templates. Available on desktop (Mac/Windows) and mobile.

### When to Use CapCut
- Social media short-form content (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)
- Quick edits with trendy caption styles and effects
- Videos where auto-captions are the primary visual element
- Templates and preset-driven editing for speed
- Mobile-first editing workflows

### CapCut Editing Workflow

**Step 1: Project Setup**
1. Open CapCut Desktop and create a new project
2. Set aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical), 16:9 (landscape), or 1:1 (square)
3. Set resolution and frame rate (1080p, 30fps for most social content)
4. Import media files to the library

**Step 2: Timeline Assembly**
1. Drag clips to the timeline in order
2. Trim clips by dragging edges or using the split tool (Ctrl/Cmd+B)
3. Rearrange clips by dragging on the timeline
4. Adjust clip speed: right-click > Speed > Normal or Curve (for dramatic speed ramping)
5. Use "Auto Reframe" to convert 16:9 footage to 9:16 — AI tracks the subject

**Step 3: Text and Captions**
1. **Auto Captions**: Text tab > Auto Captions > Generate
2. Choose caption template — CapCut has dozens of animated styles
3. Customize: font (Montserrat Bold recommended), color (white with black outline for readability), size, animation style
4. Popular caption styles: word-by-word highlight (changes color as each word is spoken), karaoke-style, bouncing text
5. Manual text: Text tab > Add Text for custom overlays, titles, and lower thirds

**Step 4: Effects and Transitions**
1. Transitions: Place between clips. Use "Fade" or "Slide" for professional look, avoid overusing flashy transitions
2. Filters: Apply LUTs or preset filters for consistent color look
3. Effects: Body effects (background blur, edge glow), video effects (glitch, zoom, shake)
4. Stickers: Use sparingly — emoji reactions, arrows, highlights
5. Keyframe animations: Click the diamond icon to set position, scale, or opacity keyframes

**Step 5: Audio**
1. Import music or use CapCut's built-in royalty-free music library
2. Adjust volume: Keep music at 15-20% under narration, 40-60% during visual-only sections
3. Use "Beat Sync" to auto-align cuts to music beats
4. Add sound effects from the built-in SFX library
5. Audio ducking: Right-click audio > Volume > Enable auto-ducking (lowers music when speech is detected)

**Step 6: Export**
1. Click Export (top-right)
2. Resolution: 1080p (2K) or 4K
3. Frame Rate: 30fps or 60fps
4. Quality: Recommended or Higher (higher increases file size significantly)
5. Format: MP4
6. Enable "Export with captions" if using auto-captions
7. Direct upload to TikTok is available from the export dialog

## DaVinci Resolve — Professional Editing and Color Grading

DaVinci Resolve is a professional-grade NLE with the industry's best color grading tools. The free version includes nearly all features — the Studio version ($295 one-time) adds GPU acceleration, HDR tools, and collaboration features.

### When to Use DaVinci Resolve
- Videos requiring professional color grading
- Complex multi-track editing with advanced audio
- Projects with mixed media (screen recordings, camera footage, animations)
- Long-form content with multiple sequences
- When you need Fairlight audio post-production
- Any project where final quality is the top priority

### DaVinci Resolve Page Workflow

DaVinci Resolve uses a page-based workflow — each page handles a different phase:

**Media Page — Import and Organize**
1. Import all media into the Media Pool
2. Create bins (folders) for: Screen Recordings, B-Roll, Audio, Graphics, Music
3. Review clips and set in/out points for usable sections
4. Add metadata and markers for organization
5. Create a new timeline for your project

**Cut Page — Fast Assembly (Optional)**
1. Use the Cut page for quick rough cuts
2. Drag clips to the timeline in order
3. Use Source Tape mode to scrub through all imported media as one long clip
4. Smart trim tools for fast, intuitive editing
5. Best for initial assembly before moving to the Edit page

**Edit Page — Detailed Editing**
1. Arrange clips on the timeline with full track control
2. Use the blade tool (B) to split clips at the playhead
3. Trim clips precisely with ripple and roll edits
4. Add transitions: Effects Library > Toolbox > Video Transitions
   - Recommended: Cross Dissolve (0.5s), Dip to Color (black, 0.3s)
   - Avoid: Star wipes, page peels, and other novelty transitions
5. Add titles: Effects Library > Toolbox > Titles
   - "Text+" for fully customizable animated text
   - "Fusion Title" for pre-built animated title templates
6. Keyframe animations: Inspector panel, click the diamond icon next to any parameter
7. Speed changes: Right-click clip > Change Clip Speed, or use the Retime Controls

**Fusion Page — Motion Graphics and VFX**
1. Create custom title animations
2. Build particle effects, 3D text, and procedural graphics
3. Node-based compositing for advanced visual effects
4. Useful for: animated logos, custom transitions, data visualizations
5. Learning curve is steep but capability is unmatched in a free tool

**Color Page — Color Correction and Grading**
1. **Primary Correction** (first node):
   - White balance: Use the eyedropper on something neutral (white/gray)
   - Exposure: Adjust lift (shadows), gamma (midtones), gain (highlights)
   - Use the waveform scope to ensure proper exposure (0-100 IRE range)
2. **Secondary Correction** (additional nodes):
   - Qualifier tool: Select and adjust specific colors (e.g., make the sky more blue)
   - Power Windows: Isolate areas of the frame for targeted adjustments
   - HSL curves for precise color control
3. **Look/Grade** (final node):
   - Apply a LUT (Look-Up Table) for a cinematic or branded look
   - Recommended free LUTs for product videos: "Clean Tech" or "Modern Corporate"
   - Adjust LUT intensity with the Key Output Gain slider
4. **Consistency**: Copy color grades between clips with right-click > Apply Grade
5. **Gallery**: Save grades as stills for reuse across projects

**Fairlight Page — Audio Post-Production**
1. Normalize all dialogue tracks to -16 LUFS (broadcast standard) or -14 LUFS (streaming)
2. Apply noise reduction: right-click audio clip > Normalize Audio Levels
3. EQ: Cut below 80Hz (removes rumble), gentle boost at 2-5kHz (presence)
4. Compression: 3:1 ratio, -20dB threshold for narration
5. Music bed: Import and adjust volume. Use automation (click the wave icon on the track header) to duck under narration
6. Master output: Limiter at -1dB to prevent clipping

**Deliver Page — Export**
1. Select a preset or configure custom settings:
   - **YouTube**: H.264, 1920x1080, 30/60fps, 10-15 Mbps, AAC 320kbps
   - **Twitter**: H.264, 1920x1080, 30fps, 8 Mbps, AAC 256kbps
   - **Instagram**: H.264, 1080x1920 (vertical), 30fps, 8 Mbps
   - **Web/Landing Page**: H.264, 1920x1080, 30fps, 5 Mbps (smaller file)
   - **Archive/Master**: ProRes 422 HQ or DNxHR HQ for maximum quality
2. Render: Add to Render Queue > Start Render
3. Verify output by playing the exported file in VLC or QuickTime

## FFmpeg — Command-Line Video Processing

FFmpeg is the Swiss Army knife of video processing. Use it for batch operations, format conversion, trimming, concatenation, subtitle burning, and automated video pipelines. It runs on all platforms and is completely free.

### When to Use FFmpeg
- Batch converting files between formats
- Trimming clips without re-encoding (instant, lossless)
- Concatenating multiple clips into one file
- Burning subtitles into video
- Extracting audio from video
- Compressing files for web delivery
- Automated video processing pipelines (CI/CD for video)
- Creating GIFs from video clips
- Adding watermarks or overlays

### Essential FFmpeg Commands

**Get video information:**
```bash
ffprobe -v quiet -print_format json -show_format -show_streams input.mp4
```

**Trim without re-encoding (instant, lossless):**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -ss 00:00:05.000 -to 00:00:30.000 -c copy trimmed.mp4
```
Note: `-ss` before `-i` is faster but less accurate. After `-i` is frame-accurate.

**Trim with re-encoding (frame-accurate):**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -ss 00:00:05.000 -to 00:00:30.000 -c:v libx264 -crf 18 -c:a aac -b:a 192k trimmed.mp4
```

**Concatenate clips (same codec):**
```bash
# Create a text file listing clips
printf "file '%s'\n" clip1.mp4 clip2.mp4 clip3.mp4 > concat_list.txt

# Concatenate without re-encoding
ffmpeg -f concat -safe 0 -i concat_list.txt -c copy output.mp4
```

**Concatenate clips (different codecs — requires re-encoding):**
```bash
ffmpeg -i clip1.mp4 -i clip2.mp4 -i clip3.mp4 \
  -filter_complex "[0:v:0][0:a:0][1:v:0][1:a:0][2:v:0][2:a:0]concat=n=3:v=1:a=1[outv][outa]" \
  -map "[outv]" -map "[outa]" output.mp4
```

**Burn in SRT subtitles:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 \
  -vf "subtitles=subs.srt:force_style='FontName=Inter,FontSize=24,PrimaryColour=&Hffffff,OutlineColour=&H000000,BorderStyle=3,Outline=2,Shadow=1,MarginV=40'" \
  -c:v libx264 -crf 18 -c:a copy output.mp4
```

**Burn in ASS subtitles (more styling control):**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -vf "ass=subs.ass" -c:v libx264 -crf 18 -c:a copy output.mp4
```

**Compress for web (H.264, good quality, small file):**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 \
  -c:v libx264 -crf 23 -preset medium \
  -c:a aac -b:a 128k \
  -movflags +faststart \
  output_web.mp4
```
CRF values: 18 = visually lossless, 23 = good quality, 28 = smaller file, lower quality.

**Convert 16:9 to 9:16 vertical with blurred background:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 \
  -filter_complex "[0:v]scale=1080:1920:force_original_aspect_ratio=decrease,pad=1080:1920:(ow-iw)/2:(oh-ih)/2[fg];[0:v]scale=1080:1920,boxblur=20[bg];[bg][fg]overlay=(W-w)/2:(H-h)/2" \
  -c:v libx264 -crf 20 -c:a copy output_vertical.mp4
```

**Add background music at 15% volume:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i video.mp4 -i music.mp3 \
  -filter_complex "[1:a]volume=0.15[music];[0:a][music]amix=inputs=2:duration=first:dropout_transition=2[outa]" \
  -map 0:v -map "[outa]" -c:v copy -c:a aac -b:a 192k output.mp4
```

**Add a PNG watermark/logo:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -i logo.png \
  -filter_complex "[1:v]scale=120:-1[logo];[0:v][logo]overlay=W-w-20:H-h-20" \
  -c:v libx264 -crf 20 -c:a copy output.mp4
```
Position options: `20:20` (top-left), `W-w-20:20` (top-right), `(W-w)/2:(H-h)/2` (center).

**Extract audio as WAV:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -vn -acodec pcm_s16le -ar 44100 -ac 2 output.wav
```

**Create high-quality GIF:**
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -ss 2 -t 5 \
  -vf "fps=15,scale=480:-1:flags=lanczos,split[s0][s1];[s0]palettegen=max_colors=128[p];[s1][p]paletteuse=dither=bayer" \
  output.gif
```

**Generate thumbnail from video:**
```bash
# Single thumbnail at specific timestamp
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -ss 00:00:15 -vframes 1 -q:v 2 thumbnail.jpg

# Grid of thumbnails (4x4 montage)
ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -vf "select='not(mod(n,300))',scale=320:180,tile=4x4" -frames:v 1 montage.jpg
```

**Batch convert all MOV files to MP4:**
```bash
for f in *.mov; do
  ffmpeg -i "$f" -c:v libx264 -crf 20 -c:a aac -b:a 192k "${f%.mov}.mp4"
done
```

**Create a looping video for landing pages:**
```bash
# Loop 3 times
ffmpeg -stream_loop 3 -i input.mp4 -c copy looped.mp4
```

## SRT Subtitle Format Reference

### Format Specification
```
[Sequence number]
[Start time] --> [End time]
[Subtitle text - line 1]
[Subtitle text - line 2 (optional)]

[blank line separating entries]
```

### Timestamp Format
`HH:MM:SS,mmm` — Hours:Minutes:Seconds,Milliseconds

### Complete SRT Example
```srt
1
00:00:00,500 --> 00:00:03,200
What if deploying to production
was as easy as clicking a button?

2
00:00:03,800 --> 00:00:06,500
With Acme Deploy, it is.

3
00:00:07,000 --> 00:00:10,200
Just connect your repo,
configure once,

4
00:00:10,500 --> 00:00:13,000
and click Deploy.

5
00:00:13,500 --> 00:00:16,800
Your code is live in seconds.
Not minutes. Seconds.

6
00:00:17,200 --> 00:00:20,500
Zero config. Zero downtime.
Zero stress.

7
00:00:21,000 --> 00:00:24,000
Join 10,000 teams shipping
faster with Acme Deploy.

8
00:00:24,500 --> 00:00:27,000
Try it free at acmedeploy.com
```

### Subtitle Best Practices
1. Break lines at natural speech pauses, not arbitrary character counts
2. Keep each subtitle on screen for at least 1 second, no more than 7 seconds
3. Leave a 200ms gap between consecutive subtitles
4. Never split a proper noun, number, or key phrase across two subtitle blocks
5. Use sentence case, not ALL CAPS (unless it is a brand name)
6. Avoid punctuation at the end of the last line unless it is a question mark
7. For emphasis, use italics sparingly: `<i>This changes everything.</i>`
8. Maximum 2 lines per subtitle, 42 characters per line

## Color Grading Basics for Product Videos

### The Goal
Color grading for product videos is not about cinematic drama — it is about making the product look its best while maintaining visual consistency across all footage.

### Step-by-Step Process
1. **White Balance**: Ensure whites are truly white. Use the eyedropper on a white UI element.
2. **Exposure**: Set proper brightness. Screen recordings should match the actual screen brightness.
3. **Contrast**: Add gentle contrast to prevent the video from looking flat. Do not crush blacks.
4. **Saturation**: Keep natural. Boost by 5-10% maximum. Over-saturated UI colors look artificial.
5. **Consistency**: Match all clips visually. A screen recording and a talking head shot should feel like they belong in the same video.

### Product Video Color Profiles
| Content Type | Temperature | Contrast | Saturation | Look |
|-------------|-------------|----------|------------|------|
| SaaS/Dev Tool | Slightly cool (6200K) | Medium | Neutral | Clean, modern |
| Consumer App | Warm (6800K) | Medium-high | +10% | Friendly, inviting |
| Enterprise | Neutral (6500K) | Low-medium | Neutral | Professional, trustworthy |
| Creative Tool | Warm (7000K) | Medium | +15% | Vibrant, expressive |
| Fintech | Cool (6000K) | Medium | -5% | Sophisticated, secure |

### Screen Recording Specific Notes
- Do not color grade the UI itself — the product's colors must be accurate
- Grade the background, device frame, and any lifestyle footage
- Ensure dark mode UIs have true blacks (check with a waveform scope)
- Light mode UIs should have clean whites without any color cast
- If mixing screen recordings with camera footage, match the camera footage to the screen recordings, not the other way around
