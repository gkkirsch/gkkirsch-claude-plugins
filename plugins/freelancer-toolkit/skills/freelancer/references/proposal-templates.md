# Proposal Templates & Best Practices

Three complete, ready-to-use proposal templates and expert guidance on writing proposals that win.

---

## Template 1: Simple Proposal (Under $5K)

For small, well-defined projects. 1-2 pages. Fast to send, easy for the client to approve.

```markdown
# Proposal: [Project Name]

**Prepared for**: [Client Name]
**Prepared by**: [Your Name]
**Date**: [Date]
**Valid until**: [Date + 14 days]

---

## The Problem

[2-3 sentences. Describe the client's situation and the pain point in THEIR words. Show that you listened.]

Example: "Your current website was built 4 years ago and doesn't reflect the quality of your work. Potential clients are bouncing within seconds, and you're losing business to competitors with modern web presence."

## The Solution

[3-5 sentences. What you'll build and the outcome it delivers. Focus on what changes for the client, not the technical details.]

Example: "I'll redesign and rebuild your website as a fast, modern, mobile-first site that showcases your portfolio and converts visitors into leads. The new site will load in under 2 seconds, rank better on Google, and include a contact form that feeds directly into your CRM."

## Deliverables

| # | Deliverable | Description |
|---|------------|-------------|
| 1 | Homepage Design | Custom design based on your brand guidelines |
| 2 | 4 Interior Pages | About, Services, Portfolio, Contact |
| 3 | Mobile Responsive | Optimized for all screen sizes |
| 4 | Contact Form | Integrated with [CRM/email] |
| 5 | SEO Basics | Meta tags, sitemap, page speed optimization |
| 6 | Launch Support | Deployment and 7-day post-launch support |

## Timeline

| Week | What Happens |
|------|-------------|
| Week 1 | Design mockups delivered for review |
| Week 2 | Revisions + development begins |
| Week 3 | Development complete, your review |
| Week 4 | Final revisions + launch |

## Investment

**$[amount]**

- 50% ($[amount]) due upfront to begin
- 50% ($[amount]) due upon delivery

## Next Steps

1. Reply "approved" or sign below
2. I'll send a deposit invoice within 24 hours
3. We start on [date]

Questions? Reply to this email or call me at [phone].

---

[Your Name]
[Email] | [Phone] | [Website]
```

**When to use**: Landing pages, small website updates, single-feature additions, quick consulting engagements, logo/branding projects.

## Template 2: Standard Proposal ($5K–$50K)

For mid-size projects. 3-5 pages. Professional structure with options and social proof.

```markdown
# [Project Name]
## Proposal for [Client Company]

**Prepared by**: [Your Name / Company]
**Date**: [Date]
**Proposal #**: [YYYY-NNN]
**Valid until**: [Date + 30 days]

---

## Executive Summary

[3-4 sentences that answer: What's the problem? What's the solution? What's the outcome? What's the investment?]

Example: "TechStart needs a customer-facing dashboard that consolidates data from three internal systems into a single view. I'll design and build a React-based dashboard with real-time data sync, role-based access, and export capabilities. Based on your usage projections, this will save your support team approximately 15 hours per week in manual report generation. The investment is $28,000 over 10 weeks."

## Understanding Your Needs

[4-6 sentences restating the client's situation. This section proves you listened and understand their business context. Use their terminology.]

### Objectives

1. **[Primary]**: [Tied to a business metric — e.g., "Reduce support ticket volume by 30% through self-service reporting"]
2. **[Secondary]**: [e.g., "Consolidate 3 data sources into a single interface"]
3. **[Tertiary]**: [e.g., "Enable real-time visibility for executive stakeholders"]

## Proposed Approach

### How I Work

[3-5 sentences describing your methodology. Make it sound proven and systematic.]

Example: "I use a phased approach that starts with a focused discovery sprint to nail down requirements before any code is written. Each phase ends with a working deliverable you can test, not a slide deck. This keeps the project grounded in reality and gives you control over the direction at every stage."

- **Deliverable**: Working application on staging

### Phase 3: Polish & Launch ([X] weeks)

### Not Included

- Content creation or copywriting
```

## Template 3: Enterprise Proposal ($50K+)

For large engagements. 5-8 pages. Includes governance, risk assessment, and success metrics.

Use the Standard template as a base and add these sections after "Timeline":

```markdown
## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Our Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Requirements evolve during development | Medium | Medium | Agile phases with check-ins; change order process for scope shifts |
| Third-party API limitations discovered | Low | High | Prototype integration in Phase 1; fallback strategy documented |
| Key stakeholder availability | Medium | High | Designated POC with decision authority; async approval via email |
| Data migration complexity exceeds estimate | Medium | Medium | Discovery phase includes data audit; buffer included in timeline |

## Governance & Communication

| Activity | Frequency | Format | Participants |
|----------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Status report | Weekly (Fridays) | Written email | PM ↔ Client POC |
| Sprint review | Bi-weekly | 30-min video call | Full team |
```

## Winning Proposal Structure

Research on what makes proposals win (from freelancer communities, agency sales data, and buyer surveys):

### The 5 Things Clients Look For

1. **"They understand my problem"** — The problem statement matters more than the solution. If the client feels heard, they trust you.
2. **"I can see exactly what I'm getting"** — Specific deliverables with descriptions beat vague promises.
3. **"The price makes sense"** — Pricing with context (what's included, payment schedule, options) feels fair. A number without context feels arbitrary.
4. **"They've done this before"** — Social proof (case studies, testimonials, relevant experience) reduces risk.
5. **"I know what happens next"** — Clear next steps with a simple action lower the barrier to saying yes.

### Structure Ordering (Optimal Flow)

1. **Problem** (emotional connection) — "I see you"
2. **Solution** (logical appeal) — "Here's how I'll fix it"
3. **Proof** (risk reduction) — "I've done this before"
4. **Price** (investment frame) — "Here's what it costs"
5. **Action** (momentum) — "Here's what to do next"

Most freelancers put pricing first or skip the problem statement. Don't.

## Handling Objections in Proposals

### "Can you do it for less?"
Include two options in your proposal. Option A (recommended, full scope) and Option B (reduced scope, lower price). The client chooses between YOUR options rather than negotiating against your single price.

### "What if it takes longer than expected?"
Fixed-price proposals include your buffer already. State this: "This price covers the full scope regardless of timeline. If scope changes are needed, we'll discuss a change order."

### "Why should I pick you over the other proposals?"
Your "Why Me" section should answer this proactively. Focus on: relevant experience, specific results, your process, and what makes working with you low-risk.

### "I need to think about it"
Your proposal should have a validity date (14-30 days). Follow up on day 3 with "Any questions I can answer?" Follow up on day 7 with a brief check-in. After 14 days, reach out with "I'm planning my upcoming schedule — is this project still on your radar?"

## Social Proof Formats

### Mini Case Study (Best for Proposals)
```
**[Client Name]** — [Project Type]
[One sentence about the challenge.]
[One sentence about what you did.]
[One sentence about the measurable result.]
```

Example:
> **TechStart Inc.** — SaaS Dashboard
> Their support team was spending 20+ hours/week generating manual reports for customers.
> I built a self-service analytics dashboard with real-time data from their existing systems.
> Support tickets dropped 45% in the first month, saving an estimated $8,000/month.

### Testimonial Format
```
> "[Specific, results-oriented quote from the client]"
> — [Full Name], [Title], [Company]
```

Example:
> "The dashboard paid for itself in the first month. Our support team finally has time to focus on actual customer problems instead of pulling reports."
> — Sarah Chen, VP of Operations, TechStart Inc.

## Terms & Conditions Boilerplate

Standard terms to include at the end of every proposal:

```markdown
## Standard Terms

1. **Scope**: This proposal covers only the work described above. Additional work requires a written change order.

2. **Payment**: Invoices are due within 14 days of receipt. Late payments accrue 1.5% monthly interest. Work may be paused on overdue invoices.

3. **Intellectual Property**: All custom work product transfers to Client upon receipt of final payment in full. Provider retains ownership of pre-existing tools and methodologies, with a perpetual license granted to Client.

4. **Revisions**: [X] rounds of revisions are included per phase. Additional revisions are billed at $[rate]/hour.

5. **Timeline**: Dates assume timely feedback from Client (within 3 business days). Delays in client response extend the timeline proportionally.

6. **Cancellation**: Either party may cancel with 14 days written notice. Client pays for all work completed to the cancellation date.

7. **Confidentiality**: Both parties agree to keep project details confidential. Provider may reference the project in their portfolio unless Client objects in writing.

8. **Limitation of Liability**: Provider's liability is limited to the total fees paid under this agreement.

9. **Warranty**: Provider warrants deliverables will function as specified for 30 days after final delivery. This warranty covers defects, not changes in requirements or third-party service issues.

10. **Independent Contractor**: Provider operates as an independent contractor, not an employee.
```

Customize per engagement. These are starting points — not legal advice. Recommend clients have their attorney review for contracts over $10K.
