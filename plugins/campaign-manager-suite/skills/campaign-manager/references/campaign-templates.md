# Campaign Templates

Five complete, ready-to-execute campaign templates with week-by-week timelines, channel allocation, creative briefs, KPIs, and budget breakdowns. Adapt these to your specific product, audience, and budget.

---

## Template 1: Product Launch Campaign

**Duration**: 8 weeks (2 weeks pre-launch + launch week + 5 weeks post-launch)
**Budget Range**: $5,000 - $50,000
**Best For**: SaaS products, consumer tech, DTC product launches, major feature releases

### Channel Mix and Budget Allocation

| Channel | % of Budget | $10K Example | Role |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------|
| Meta Ads (FB/IG) | 30% | $3,000 | Awareness + consideration |
| Google Search Ads | 25% | $2,500 | High-intent capture |
| Email Marketing | 10% | $1,000 | Nurture + announcement |
| Content/PR | 15% | $1,500 | Credibility + SEO |
| Influencer/Creator | 10% | $1,000 | Social proof + reach |
| Reserve/Contingency | 10% | $1,000 | Optimization budget |

### Week-by-Week Timeline

**Weeks -2 to -1: Pre-Launch**
- Build landing page with email capture ("Be the first to know")
- Create all ad creative (3-5 variations per channel)
- Produce 2-3 pieces of supporting content (blog post, comparison guide, how-it-works video)
- Set up all tracking: GA4 events, conversion pixels, UTM conventions
- Brief 3-5 micro-influencers with product samples/access
- Build email announcement sequence (3 emails: teaser, launch day, follow-up)
- Set up social media countdown posts
- QA everything: landing page mobile, form submissions, tracking fires

**Week 1: Launch**
- Day 1: Send launch email to full list. Publish blog post. Go live on all social channels.
- Day 1: Activate Meta Ads — start with 3 ad sets: Lookalike 1% (40% budget), Interest targeting (40%), Remarketing (20%)
- Day 1: Activate Google Search — brand keywords + 2-3 high-intent non-brand campaigns
- Day 2-3: Influencer posts go live (stagger for sustained visibility)
- Day 3: Monitor all metrics, fix any tracking issues
- Day 5: First performance check — pause any ads with CTR < 0.5% or CPC > 2x target
- Day 7: Weekly report — adjust bids, reallocate from underperformers

**Week 2: Optimize**
- Review launch week data: Which channels drove the most conversions at the lowest CPA?
- A/B test top 2 ad creatives against new variations
- Send second email to non-openers with different subject line
- Publish customer testimonial or early adopter story
- Expand Google Search to additional keyword groups if CPA is within target
- Adjust Meta audience targeting based on conversion data (check age, gender, placement breakdowns)

**Week 3-4: Scale**
- Increase budget 20-30% on top-performing channels/ad sets
- Decrease budget 30-50% on underperforming channels
- Launch remarketing campaign for landing page visitors who did not convert
- Create and publish case study or use-case content
- Test new Meta ad formats (Reels, Stories) if Feed is working
- Send targeted email to engaged-but-not-converted leads

**Week 5-6: Sustain**
- Refresh ad creative (combat creative fatigue — watch for declining CTR)
- Launch Google Display remarketing for broader reach
- Publish SEO-optimized comparison content ("Product X vs. competitors")
- Re-engage influencers for second-wave content
- Test new audience segments on Meta (broaden targeting if core segments saturating)

**Week 7-8: Wrap and Learn**
- Begin scaling back ad spend on diminishing-return channels
- Final push email to remaining unconverted leads
- Pull comprehensive performance data across all channels
- Conduct post-mortem analysis (see template below)
- Document learnings for next campaign
- Transition any ongoing tactics to always-on campaigns

### KPIs and Targets (Example for $10K Budget)

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Landing page visitors | 15,000 | GA4 |
| Email signups (pre-launch) | 500 | ESP |
| Free trials / purchases | 150 | Product analytics |
| Blended CPA | $67 | Total spend / conversions |
| ROAS | 3x+ | Revenue / ad spend |
| Email open rate (launch) | 35%+ | ESP |
| Social engagement rate | 3%+ | Platform analytics |

### Creative Brief: Launch Video Ad (Meta)

- **Objective**: Drive landing page visits and trial signups
- **Target**: Awareness-stage prospects who match ICP
- **Key message**: "[Product] is now live — [one-sentence value prop]"
- **Format**: 15-30 second vertical video (9:16)
- **Hook (first 3 seconds)**: Lead with the problem, not the solution. "Tired of [pain point]?"
- **Body**: Show the product solving the problem in 3 quick cuts
- **CTA**: "Start your free trial — link in bio" or "Try it free today"
- **Tone**: Energetic, confident, not overly salesy

### Launch Checklist

- [ ] Landing page live and tested (desktop + mobile + tablet)
- [ ] Page load time < 3 seconds (test with PageSpeed Insights)
- [ ] All forms submitting correctly and routing to CRM/ESP
- [ ] Conversion tracking verified: GA4, Meta pixel, Google Ads tag
- [ ] UTM parameters applied to all campaign links
- [ ] Email sequences built, tested with seed list, scheduled
- [ ] Ad creative uploaded and approved in all platforms
- [ ] Budget loaded and daily caps set correctly
- [ ] Remarketing audiences created and populated
- [ ] Social media posts drafted and scheduled
- [ ] Influencer content reviewed and approved
- [ ] Analytics dashboard configured with key metrics
- [ ] Team notified of launch date and responsibilities
- [ ] Contingency plan documented (what to do if CPAs are 2x target)

## Template 2: Lead Generation Campaign

**Duration**: Ongoing (plan in 30-day sprints)
**Budget Range**: $2,000 - $10,000/month
**Best For**: B2B SaaS, professional services, high-ticket products, enterprise software

### Channel Mix and Budget Allocation

| Channel | % of Budget | $5K/mo Example | Role |
|---------|-----------|---------------|------|
| Google Search Ads | 35% | $1,750 | Capture high-intent searches |
| LinkedIn Ads | 25% | $1,250 | B2B targeting + lead gen forms |
| Content/SEO | 15% | $750 | Organic pipeline |
| Email Nurture | 10% | $500 | Lead-to-MQL conversion |
| Meta Remarketing | 5% | $250 | Retarget website visitors |
| Reserve | 10% | $500 | Testing + optimization |

### Month 1: Foundation

**Week 1-2: Setup**
- Define ICP: Job title, company size, industry, budget authority, pain points
- Create primary lead magnet (whitepaper, template, calculator, or webinar)
- Build dedicated landing page with lead gen form (name, email, company, role — 4 fields max)
- Set up CRM integration and lead scoring model
- Configure Google Search campaigns: 3 campaigns (brand, solution-aware, problem-aware)
- Configure LinkedIn campaigns: 2 campaigns (Sponsored Content + Lead Gen Forms)
- Build 5-email nurture sequence for new leads
- Set up lead-to-MQL scoring criteria

**Week 3-4: Launch and Initial Data**
- Launch all paid campaigns
- Publish 2 SEO-targeted blog posts supporting the lead magnet topic
- Begin outbound promotion of lead magnet via email to existing contacts
- Monitor daily: CPL by channel, form completion rate, email delivery
- Goal: 30-50 leads to validate channel performance and lead quality

### Month 2-3: Optimize

- Analyze lead quality by channel (what % became MQLs? SQLs?)
- Shift budget toward channels producing highest-quality leads (not just cheapest CPL)
- A/B test landing page (test headline, form length, social proof)
- Refresh ad creative based on top-performing messaging
- Expand Google Search keywords based on search term report
- Create second lead magnet for different funnel stage
- Launch webinar campaign as mid-funnel conversion event
- Set up lead scoring alerts for sales-ready leads

### Ongoing Monthly Rhythm

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Week 1 | Review prior month performance, plan optimizations |
| Week 2 | Implement optimization changes, refresh creative |
| Week 3 | Create new content, test new audiences/keywords |
| Week 4 | Full performance review, budget reallocation, reporting |

### KPIs and Targets (Example for $5K/month)

| Metric | Month 1 Target | Month 3 Target | Month 6 Target |
|--------|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| Marketing-Qualified Leads | 30 | 60 | 100 |
| Cost per Lead (CPL) | $100 | $75 | $50 |
| Lead-to-MQL Rate | 25% | 35% | 40% |
| MQL-to-SQL Rate | 20% | 30% | 35% |
| Pipeline Value Generated | $30K | $90K | $200K |
| Content Organic Traffic | 500 | 2,000 | 5,000 |

### Lead Scoring Model

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Downloaded lead magnet | +10 |
| Visited pricing page | +15 |
| Attended webinar | +20 |
| Opened 3+ emails | +5 |
| Clicked email CTA | +10 |
| Visited 5+ pages in one session | +10 |
| Job title matches ICP | +15 |
| Company size matches ICP | +10 |
| Returned to site 3+ times | +10 |
| **MQL threshold** | **50 points** |
| Requested demo or contact | +30 (auto-SQL) |

## Template 3: Brand Awareness Campaign

**Duration**: 12 weeks
**Budget Range**: $10,000 - $100,000
**Best For**: New market entry, rebranding, category creation, building mental availability

### Channel Mix and Budget Allocation

| Channel | % of Budget | $25K Example | Role |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------|
| YouTube Ads | 25% | $6,250 | Video reach + brand storytelling |
| Meta Ads (FB/IG) | 25% | $6,250 | Broad reach + frequency |
| TikTok | 10% | $2,500 | Young demographic reach |
| Display/Programmatic | 10% | $2,500 | Reach extension + remarketing |
| Content/PR | 15% | $3,750 | Credibility + earned media |
| Influencer | 10% | $2,500 | Authentic endorsement |
| Reserve | 5% | $1,250 | Optimization |

### Measurement Framework

Brand awareness is harder to measure than direct response. Use these proxy metrics:

**Primary metrics:**
- Brand search volume (Google Trends, Search Console) — target 30%+ increase
- Aided brand awareness (survey) — target 15%+ increase
- Social share of voice — target 10%+ share in your category

**Secondary metrics:**
- Video view rate and completion rate
- Social media reach and impressions (deduplicated if possible)
- Website direct traffic (people typing your URL directly)
- Branded search click-through rate

**How to measure brand lift:**
- Run pre/post brand awareness survey (Google Consumer Surveys, SurveyMonkey, or platform-native brand lift studies)
- Track branded search volume weekly in Google Search Console
- Monitor social listening for brand mentions (Brandwatch, Mention, or even free Google Alerts)
- Compare direct traffic before vs. during vs. after campaign

### Week-by-Week Timeline

**Weeks 1-2: Foundation**
- Develop brand messaging framework and creative concept
- Produce hero video (30-60 seconds) and cutdowns (15s, 6s bumper)
- Create static/carousel ad variants for Meta
- Brief PR agency or internal team on narrative
- Brief 5-8 influencers with brand story and creative guidelines
- Set up brand lift study (if budget allows)
- Baseline all metrics: brand search volume, direct traffic, social following

**Weeks 3-4: Launch**
- Launch YouTube: Skippable in-stream (30s hero video) + Bumper (6s reach)
- Launch Meta: Video-first with reach objective, broad targeting
- Launch TikTok: Spark Ads boosting creator content
- First wave of influencer content goes live
- First PR placement or press release distributed
- Monitor frequency: ensure 2-3 average frequency per week (not more)

**Weeks 5-8: Sustain**
- Maintain consistent presence across all channels
- Rotate creative every 2-3 weeks to prevent fatigue
- Second wave of influencer content
- Publish thought leadership content (LinkedIn articles, guest posts)
- Monitor brand search volume for lift vs. baseline
- Adjust channel mix based on reach efficiency (CPM and unique reach)

**Weeks 9-10: Amplify**
- Increase spend on highest-reach channels by 20%
- Launch remarketing campaign targeting video viewers (YouTube + Meta)
- Push highest-performing content across additional distribution channels
- Third wave of influencer or creator content

**Weeks 11-12: Measure and Transition**
- Run post-campaign brand awareness survey
- Compare all metrics to baseline
- Comprehensive brand lift analysis
- Decide what to continue as always-on (typically YouTube remarketing + social)
- Document brand assets created for future use

## Template 4: E-commerce Sales Campaign

**Duration**: 4-6 weeks (seasonal or promotional)
**Budget Range**: $5,000 - $25,000
**Best For**: Seasonal sales, product drops, holiday campaigns, clearance events

### Channel Mix and Budget Allocation

| Channel | % of Budget | $15K Example | Role |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------|
| Meta Ads (FB/IG) | 30% | $4,500 | Prospecting + DPA remarketing |
| Google Shopping | 25% | $3,750 | High-intent purchase capture |
| Google Search | 10% | $1,500 | Brand + sale-term capture |
| Email Marketing | 15% | $2,250 | List monetization + urgency |
| TikTok/Influencer | 10% | $1,500 | Social proof + trend riding |
| Reserve | 10% | $1,500 | Flash sale boost + optimization |

### Pre-Campaign Prep (2 Weeks Before)

- **Product feed optimization**: Update Google Merchant Center with sale prices, availability, high-quality images
- **Audience building**: Create custom audiences (past purchasers by category, high-AOV customers, cart abandoners, email subscribers)
- **Lookalike creation**: Build lookalikes from best customers (top 25% by LTV)
- **Creative production**: Lifestyle shots, product flatlays, UGC videos, sale announcement graphics
- **Email segmentation**: Segment list into VIP (early access), engaged, and broad
- **Site prep**: Sale landing page, updated banners, promotional header bar, urgency elements (countdown timer, stock indicators)
- **Tracking**: Verify all product-level revenue tracking, set up sale-specific UTM parameters

### Campaign Timeline

**Day -3 to -1: Pre-Sale Tease**
- Email VIP segment: Early access announcement
- Social media teaser posts (countdowns, sneak peeks)
- Build anticipation without revealing exact discounts

**Day 1-2: Launch**
- Send sale announcement email to full list (subject line A/B test)
- Activate all paid campaigns simultaneously
- Social media posts announcing the sale
- Influencer/creator posts go live
- Monitor site performance (speed, checkout flow, inventory)

**Day 3-7: Sustain**
- Send "don't miss out" email to non-openers
- Daily bid adjustments on Google Shopping (increase for high-ROAS products)
- Test new Meta ad creative (swap underperformers)
- Push best-selling items in paid and email channels
- Monitor inventory — suppress ads for out-of-stock items

**Day 8-14: Mid-Campaign Push**
- "Sale ending soon" email with countdown
- Increase remarketing budget 30% (hottest audience)
- Highlight social proof: "X items sold", customer reviews
- Test increased discounts on slow-moving inventory
- Deploy reserve budget on highest-ROAS channels

**Day 15-21: Extension/Close**
- "Last chance" email (final send)
- Increase urgency in all creative
- If performing well, extend 3-5 days with "extended by popular demand" messaging
- If underperforming, concentrate remaining budget on Google Shopping + email only
- Begin post-sale remarketing for non-converters with regular-price best sellers

### KPIs and Targets (Example for $15K Budget)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Total Revenue | $60,000+ (4x ROAS) |
| Orders | 600+ |
| Average Order Value | $100+ |
| Email Revenue | $15,000 (25% of total) |
| Paid Revenue (Meta + Google) | $40,000 (67% of total) |
| New Customer Acquisition | 200+ new customers |
| Blended CAC | $25 |
| Cart Abandonment Recovery | 12% of abandoners |
| Site Conversion Rate | 3.5%+ |

### Post-Sale Analysis

| Question | Data Source |
|----------|-----------|
| Total revenue vs. target | GA4 + Shopify/platform |
| Revenue by channel (attributed) | GA4 + platform reports |
| Best-selling products during sale | Platform analytics |
| New vs. returning customer split | CRM / platform |
| Average discount given | Platform analytics |
| Gross margin impact of discounts | Finance / accounting |
| Email list growth during sale | ESP |
| Top-performing ad creative | Meta/Google Ads |

## Template 5: SaaS Free Trial Campaign

**Duration**: Ongoing (plan in 30-day cycles)
**Budget Range**: $3,000 - $15,000/month
**Best For**: SaaS products with self-serve free trials, PLG (product-led growth) companies

### Channel Mix and Budget Allocation

| Channel | % of Budget | $8K/mo Example | Role |
|---------|-----------|---------------|------|
| Google Search Ads | 35% | $2,800 | High-intent trial signups |
| Meta Ads | 20% | $1,600 | Awareness + retargeting |
| Content/SEO | 15% | $1,200 | Organic trial pipeline |
| Email | 10% | $800 | Onboarding + trial-to-paid conversion |
| Review Sites (G2, Capterra) | 10% | $800 | High-intent comparison shoppers |
| Reserve | 10% | $800 | Testing + optimization |

### Funnel Architecture

```
VISITOR → TRIAL SIGNUP → ACTIVATION → ENGAGEMENT → CONVERSION → EXPANSION
   ↑           ↑              ↑            ↑            ↑            ↑
  Paid     Landing Page   Onboarding    In-App      Trial End    Upsell
  SEO      Signup Form     Emails      Triggers     Paywall     Campaigns
  Content                  In-App       Usage
                           Guides       Scoring
```

### Key Metrics by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Metric | Benchmark | Target |
|-------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Visitor → Trial | Signup Rate | 3-8% | 5%+ |
| Trial → Activated | Activation Rate | 40-60% | 50%+ |
| Activated → Engaged | Engagement Rate | 30-50% | 40%+ |
| Engaged → Paid | Conversion Rate | 15-30% | 20%+ |
| Overall Visitor → Paid | Full Funnel | 0.5-2% | 1%+ |

**Activation definition**: User completes 1-3 key actions that correlate with long-term retention. Define your "aha moment" — the action after which users are 3x+ more likely to convert.

### Trial Onboarding Email Sequence

| Email | Timing | Subject | Goal |
|-------|--------|---------|------|
| 1. Welcome | Immediately | "Welcome to [Product] — start here" | Guide to first key action |
| 2. Quick Win | Day 1 | "Complete your first [key action] in 2 minutes" | Drive activation |
| 3. Value Demo | Day 3 | "See what [Product] can do for you" | Show advanced features |
| 4. Social Proof | Day 5 | "How [Customer] achieved [Result] with [Product]" | Build confidence |
| 5. Check-In | Day 7 | "Need help? Here are our most-asked questions" | Reduce churn risk |
| 6. Midpoint | Day 7 (14-day trial) | "You're halfway through your trial" | Create urgency |
| 7. Convert | Day 11 | "Your trial ends in 3 days — upgrade now" | Drive conversion |
| 8. Last Chance | Day 13 | "Last day to keep your [Product] account" | Final push |
| 9. Expired | Day 15 | "We saved your data — upgrade to get it back" | Win back |
| 10. Win-Back | Day 30 | "We've added [new feature] — try again free" | Re-engagement |

### Google Search Campaign Structure

```
Campaign 1: Brand
├── Ad Group: Brand Terms — [product name], [product name] login, [product name] pricing
└── Expected: CTR 15%+, CVR 15-30%, CPC $0.50-2.00

Campaign 2: Solution-Aware (High Intent)
├── Ad Group: "[category] software" — project management software, CRM software, etc.
├── Ad Group: "best [category]" — best project management tool, best CRM for startups
└── Expected: CTR 3-6%, CVR 3-6%, CPC $3-10

Campaign 3: Problem-Aware
├── Ad Group: "how to [solve problem]" — how to manage remote teams, how to track sales
└── Expected: CTR 2-4%, CVR 1-3%, CPC $2-5

Campaign 4: Competitor
├── Ad Group: "[Competitor] alternative" — Asana alternative, Salesforce alternative
├── Ad Group: "[Competitor] vs" — Asana vs Monday, HubSpot vs Salesforce
└── Expected: CTR 2-4%, CVR 2-5%, CPC $3-15
```

### Monthly Optimization Cycle

**Week 1: Acquire**
- Review search term reports, add negatives, expand keywords
- Refresh top-of-funnel Meta creative (test new hooks)
- Publish 1-2 SEO blog posts targeting comparison/solution keywords
- Review trial signup rate by landing page — A/B test lowest converter

**Week 2: Activate**
- Analyze onboarding email performance (open rates, click rates by email)
- Review activation rate by acquisition channel (are paid users activating?)
- Test onboarding flow changes (reduce steps to aha moment)
- Identify users stuck pre-activation and trigger help intervention

**Week 3: Convert**
- Analyze trial-to-paid conversion by cohort
- Test pricing page changes (layout, plan names, feature emphasis)
- Test trial length (7 vs. 14 vs. 30 days) if you have enough traffic
- Review support tickets from trial users for common objections

**Week 4: Report and Plan**
- Full funnel report: Visitor → Trial → Activated → Paid → Revenue
- Calculate CAC by channel including trial-to-paid conversion rate
- True CAC = Cost per Trial Signup / Trial-to-Paid Conversion Rate
- Plan next month's optimizations based on biggest funnel drop-off
