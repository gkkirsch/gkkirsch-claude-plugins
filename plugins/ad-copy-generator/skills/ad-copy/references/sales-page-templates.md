# Sales Page Templates — High-Converting Landing Page Structures

Complete templates for building sales pages that convert. Based on proven direct response principles and tested across thousands of campaigns.

---

## The Complete Sales Page Blueprint

Every high-converting sales page follows this progression. Each section has a specific psychological job. Skip a section and conversions drop.

### Section 1: Hero (The 2.6-Second Decision)

**Job**: Stop the scroll. Create an open loop. Make them stay.

**Components**:
- **Headline**: The single most important element. Use a proven formula (see hook-formulas.md). Must pass the "Would I stop scrolling?" test.
- **Subheadline**: Clarifies the promise or adds a supporting benefit. 15-25 words max.
- **Hero CTA**: Button above the fold for ready buyers. "Get Started" or "Claim Your Spot" — not "Learn More."
- **Social proof chip**: One line of credibility. "Trusted by 2,847 entrepreneurs" or "4.9/5 from 500+ reviews."

**15 Proven Headline Formulas**:

1. **Direct Promise**: "How to [Outcome] in [Timeframe] Without [Pain Point]"
2. **Curiosity Gap**: "The [Number] [Adjective] Secrets That [Unexpected Result]"
3. **Problem/Solution Contrast**: "The [Negative State] vs [Positive State] Method"
4. **Authority Positioning**: "[Credibility] Reveals [Method] That [Result]"
5. **Opportunity Window**: "The [Trend] That's [Creating Results] Before [Deadline]"
6. **Transformation Story**: "How [Person] Went From [Bad] to [Good] in [Time]"
7. **Method Naming**: "The [Proprietary Name] That [Result] for [Audience]"
8. **Question Hook**: "[Provocative Question]? Here's How [Solution]"
9. **Number-Driven**: "[Number] Ways to [Outcome] (Even If [Obstacle])"
10. **Warning**: "Warning: [Common Mistake] Is Costing You [Loss]"
11. **If-Then**: "If You [Situation], You Can [Outcome] — Here's How"
12. **Testimonial Headline**: "[Name] Made [Result] in [Time]. Here's How."
13. **Contrarian**: "Everything You've Been Told About [Topic] Is Wrong"
14. **Simple Statement**: "[Bold Claim]. Period."
15. **How-To + Specificity**: "How I [Specific Result] in [Specific Time] Using [Specific Method]"

**Headline Psychology**:
- The brain processes headlines in 4 stages: RAS activation (0.1s) → Pattern recognition (0.5s) → Emotional evaluation (1.0s) → Logical assessment (1.0s)
- Odd numbers (3, 5, 7) outperform even numbers
- Specific numbers ($23,847) outperform round numbers ($24,000)
- "Without" triggers loss aversion and addresses objections simultaneously
- Questions activate the brain's problem-solving mechanisms

### Section 2: Problem (The Pain Amplifier)

**Job**: Make the status quo intolerable. Create cognitive dissonance.

**Components**:
- **Problem statement**: Name their pain in their exact words. Be brutally specific.
- **Symptom list**: 3-5 symptoms they recognize. Use "Do you..." or "Are you..." format.
- **Consequence cascade**: Show what happens if they don't solve this. Escalate from annoying to devastating.
- **Empathy bridge**: "I know because I was there too" or "I've heard this from hundreds of [audience]."

**Template**:
```
## [Pain Point] Is Costing You More Than You Think

You're [doing the wrong thing]. You know it. I know it. Here's what that looks like:

- You [symptom 1 — the daily frustration]
- You [symptom 2 — the wasted resource]
- You [symptom 3 — the emotional toll]
- You [symptom 4 — the opportunity cost]

And the worst part? Every day you don't fix this, [escalating consequence].

[Next month / next year], you'll look back and realize [the compounding cost of inaction].

I know because [empathy bridge — personal experience or client observation].
```

### Section 3: Agitate (The Knife Twist)

**Job**: Make them FEEL the problem. Move from intellectual understanding to emotional urgency.

**Components**:
- **Failed solutions**: List what they've already tried (and why it didn't work)
- **Root cause reveal**: Explain WHY those solutions failed — introduce the insight
- **Emotional escalation**: Connect the problem to deeper fears, desires, or identity
- **"But what if" transition**: Pivot from pain to possibility

**Template**:
```
## You've Tried Everything. Here's Why It Hasn't Worked.

You've probably tried:
- [Common solution 1] — but [why it fails]
- [Common solution 2] — but [why it fails]
- [Common solution 3] — but [why it fails]

None of these work because they all miss [the root cause].

The real problem isn't [surface issue]. It's [deeper issue].

And until you fix [deeper issue], you'll keep [repeating the painful cycle].

But what if there was a way to [outcome] without [the biggest objection]?
```

### Section 4: Solution (The Reveal)

**Job**: Present your product as the inevitable answer. It should feel like discovery, not a pitch.

**Components**:
- **Introduction**: Name your product and its core mechanism
- **Origin story**: Brief story of how/why you created it (builds credibility)
- **Core promise**: What it does, who it's for, and how fast it works
- **Unique mechanism**: What makes your approach different (proprietary method/framework)
- **How it works**: 3-step simplified process

**Template**:
```
## Introducing [Product Name]: [One-Line Description]

After [your journey / discovery / experience], I built [Product Name] to solve [specific problem] for [specific audience].

It works because of [Unique Mechanism Name] — a [X]-step process that [what it does differently].

Here's how it works:

**Step 1: [Action]** — [What they do and what happens]
**Step 2: [Action]** — [What they do and what happens]
**Step 3: [Action]** — [What they do and what happens]

That's it. No [common objection 1]. No [common objection 2]. Just [desired outcome].
```

### Section 5: Features & Benefits (The Value Stack)

**Job**: Build perceived value. Make the price feel like a steal.

**Rules**:
- Every feature gets a "so you can" benefit translation
- Lead with benefits, support with features
- Stack value: assign dollar values to each component
- Group into logical modules/pillars

**Template**:
```
## Here's Everything You Get Inside [Product Name]

### [Module/Component 1 Name] (Value: $XX)
[What it is] so you can [what it does for them].
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]
- [Specific deliverable 3]

### [Module/Component 2 Name] (Value: $XX)
[What it is] so you can [what it does for them].
- [Specific deliverable 1]
- [Specific deliverable 2]
- [Specific deliverable 3]

### BONUS: [Bonus Name] (Value: $XX)
[What it is] — yours free when you join today.

**Total Value: $X,XXX**
**Your Investment Today: $XX**
```

### Section 6: Social Proof (The Trust Engine)

**Job**: Prove it works for people like them. Overcome "will this work for ME?"

**Components**:
- **Testimonials**: Real quotes with names, photos, specific results. Address different objections with different testimonials.
- **Case studies**: Before → intervention → after format with specifics
- **Numbers**: Total customers, revenue generated, satisfaction rate
- **Logos/badges**: Media mentions, certifications, partnerships
- **Screenshots**: Results dashboards, messages, reviews

**Testimonial Selection Strategy**:
- Include one that addresses price objection ("best investment I ever made")
- Include one that addresses time objection ("got results in just 2 weeks")
- Include one that addresses skill objection ("I'm not tech-savvy and it still worked")
- Include one from someone similar to your ideal customer
- Include one with specific dollar amounts or metrics

**Template**:
```
## What [Number]+ [Customers/Students/Users] Are Saying

> "[Specific result quote with numbers and timeframe]"
> — [Name], [Title/Descriptor], [Location]

> "[Objection-handling quote — addresses skepticism they had]"
> — [Name], [Title/Descriptor]

> "[Transformation quote — before/after contrast]"
> — [Name], [Title/Descriptor]

**By the numbers:**
- [X]+ [customers] served
- $[X]M+ in results generated
- [X]% satisfaction rate
- [X]-star average rating
```

### Section 7: FAQ / Objection Handling

**Job**: Remove every remaining reason NOT to buy.

**Common Objections to Address**:
1. "Is this right for me?" → Describe ideal customer and show range of success stories
2. "What if it doesn't work?" → Point to guarantee + testimonials
3. "I don't have time" → Show time investment required + time saved
4. "How is this different from X?" → Explain unique mechanism
5. "I'm not [technical/experienced/ready]" → Address skill level required
6. "Can I get a refund?" → Restate guarantee clearly
7. "What do I get exactly?" → List deliverables
8. "How long until I see results?" → Set realistic expectations with proof

**Template**:
```
## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is [Product] right for me if I'm [common concern]?**
A: [Direct answer]. [Product] was specifically designed for [audience], even if [limitation]. [Proof point].

**Q: What if it doesn't work for me?**
A: [Product] comes with a [X]-day money-back guarantee. If [condition], simply [process] for a full refund. [Confidence statement about why you offer this].

**Q: How quickly will I see results?**
A: Most [customers] see [initial result] within [timeframe]. [Name] saw [specific result] in [specific time]. Your results depend on [honest factors].
```

### Section 8: Risk Reversal (The Guarantee)

**Job**: Make "yes" feel safe. Remove the last barrier.

**Types**:
- **Money-back guarantee**: "Full refund within X days, no questions asked"
- **Results guarantee**: "If you don't [specific result], we'll [remedy]"
- **Better-than-money-back**: "If it doesn't work, keep everything AND we'll refund you"
- **Conditional guarantee**: "Follow the steps. If you don't see [result], we refund"

**Template**:
```
## The [Guarantee Name] Guarantee

Try [Product] for [X] days. Follow the steps. Put in the work.

If you don't [specific measurable outcome], email [address] and I'll refund every penny. No hoops. No guilt. No hard feelings.

I can offer this because [reason — confidence in the product]. [X]% of members [positive stat]. I'm betting you will too.
```

### Section 9: Final CTA (The Close)

**Job**: Restate the value, create urgency, give clear next steps.

**Components**:
- **Recap**: Everything they get (brief)
- **Price anchor**: Show value vs. price contrast
- **Urgency**: Why now (deadline, bonus expiring, price increase, limited spots)
- **CTA button**: Action-oriented text
- **Post-CTA reassurance**: Guarantee reminder, security badges

**Template**:
```
## Ready to [Desired Outcome]?

Here's everything you get today:
- [Component 1] (Value: $XX)
- [Component 2] (Value: $XX)
- [Bonus] (Value: $XX)

**Total Value: $X,XXX**

**Your price today: $XX**

[CTA BUTTON: "Get Instant Access" / "Start My Transformation" / "Claim My Spot"]

[Urgency line: "This price is available until [date]" or "Only [X] spots remaining"]

[Guarantee reminder: "Protected by our [X]-day guarantee"]

[Security: "Secure checkout powered by Stripe. Your data is encrypted and protected."]
```

## Quick-Deploy Sales Page Formats

### The Mini Sales Page (For Low-Ticket $7-$47)

```
1. Headline (Direct Promise formula)
2. Subheadline (Clarify + urgency)
3. 3-bullet problem list
4. "Here's what you get" (3-5 deliverables)
5. Price + CTA button
6. 2-3 short testimonials
7. 30-day guarantee
8. Final CTA
```

Total length: 500-800 words. One page. No scrolling fatigue.

### The Standard Sales Page (For Mid-Ticket $97-$497)

```
1. Hero (Headline + subheadline + CTA)
2. Problem section
3. Agitate section
4. Solution reveal
5. Features/benefits (4-6 modules)
6. Social proof (5-8 testimonials)
7. Pricing + CTA
8. FAQ (5-8 questions)
9. Guarantee
10. Final CTA with urgency
```

Total length: 2,000-4,000 words.

### The Long-Form Sales Page (For High-Ticket $497+)

```
1. Hero with video
2. Story/origin section
3. Problem deep-dive
4. Agitate with failed solutions
5. Solution + unique mechanism
6. Full curriculum/feature breakdown
7. Social proof section (10+ testimonials)
8. Case studies (2-3 detailed)
9. "Who this is for / not for"
10. Value stack with pricing
11. Bonuses
12. Guarantee
13. FAQ (8-12 questions)
14. Final CTA
15. P.S. section (urgency + reminder)
```

Total length: 5,000-10,000+ words.

## The PERC Method for Content Structure

Each section of your sales page should apply PERC:

- **P — Plan**: Lay out a simple, clear path for the reader
- **E — Eliminate**: Remove obstacles, wrong steps, and complexity
- **R — Replace**: Insert smarter systems, tools, and frameworks
- **C — Create**: Help them build or launch something that triggers transformation

This ensures every section moves the reader closer to a real result, not just "teaches."

## Conversion Optimization Checklist

Before publishing any sales page, verify:

- [ ] Headline passes the "Would I stop scrolling?" test
- [ ] Problem section uses the prospect's exact language
- [ ] Every feature has a "so you can" benefit
- [ ] At least 3 testimonials addressing different objections
- [ ] Guarantee is prominent and specific
- [ ] CTA appears at least 3 times (top, middle, bottom)
- [ ] Price is anchored against higher value
- [ ] Urgency is legitimate (not fake scarcity)
- [ ] FAQ addresses top 5 objections
- [ ] Mobile-friendly formatting (short paragraphs, subheadings)
- [ ] One clear CTA action (don't split attention)
- [ ] P.S. section restates core promise + deadline
