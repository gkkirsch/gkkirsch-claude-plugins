# Hook Formulas — Attention-Grabbing Opening Lines

Hooks are the single most important element of any ad. You have 2.6 seconds to stop the scroll. These formulas are proven across millions of dollars in ad spend.

---

## The Triple Hook Formula

The most reliable hook structure for video and text ads. Three elements, each doing a different psychological job:

1. **Premise**: A bold claim or statement that demands attention
2. **Stakes**: Why it matters — what's at risk
3. **Twist**: A pattern interrupt that creates an irresistible curiosity gap

### Triple Hook Templates

```
"[Bold claim about results], [why that matters to you], [surprising constraint that makes it seem impossible]."

"[Specific number/result], [emotional stakes], [and the unexpected way it happened]."

"[Catchy opening statement], [the what/premise], [the obstacle I'm doing it in spite of]."
```

## Category Hook Formulas

### Pain-Point Hooks

Designed to make the reader feel the problem viscerally.

```
"Still [doing painful thing]? There's a reason it's not working."
"If you're [suffering from X], stop what you're doing and read this."
"You're losing [specific amount/resource] every [time period] because of [problem]."
"[Number]% of [audience] make this mistake. It costs them [specific loss]."
"Tired of [painful activity] with nothing to show for it?"
"Warning: [Common practice] is silently killing your [results/business/health]."
```

### Curiosity Hooks

Create an information gap the brain must close.

```
"The [number] [adjective] secrets that [unexpected result]."
"I asked [authority figure] one question. Their answer surprised me."
"There's a reason [successful people] never talk about this."
"What [industry] doesn't want you to know about [topic]."
"I found a [thing] hidden in plain sight that changes everything about [topic]."
"The counterintuitive reason [unexpected cause] leads to [unexpected effect]."
```

### Dream-State Hooks

Activate desire by painting the aspirational future.

```
"What would you do with an extra [amount] per month?"
"Imagine waking up tomorrow and [dream scenario]."
"[Timeframe] from now, you could be [desired state]. Here's how."
"This is what [result] actually looks like in real life."
"People who [achieve X] all have one thing in common."
"[Specific person] just [achieved dream result] and they started [where your audience is]."
```

### Contrarian Hooks

Challenge conventional wisdom to create a pattern interrupt.

```
"Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong."
"[Popular advice] is the worst thing you can do for [goal]."
"I stopped [common practice] and my [results] doubled."
"Unpopular opinion: [contrarian take]. Here's the data."
"The [topic] advice that worked in [year] will bankrupt you in [current year]."
"[Number] reasons to STOP [common practice] immediately."
```

### Social Proof Hooks

Leverage the power of others' success.

```
"[Name] went from [bad state] to [good state] in [time]. Here's how."
"[Number]+ [people] have already [achieved result] with this."
"This [thing] has generated $[amount] for my students. And it's free."
"Why are [impressive number] people switching to [method]?"
"[Specific person] tried it. [Timeframe] later: [specific result]."
"The method [famous person/company] uses to [achieve thing]."
```

### Question Hooks

Activate the brain's problem-solving circuits.

```
"What if [thing they think is true] was actually holding you back?"
"Have you ever wondered why [observation about their industry]?"
"What's the #1 thing stopping you from [goal]? (It's not what you think.)"
"Why do some [audience] succeed while most struggle?"
"What would change if you could [dream outcome] in [short time]?"
"Can you spot the $10,000 mistake in this [ad/page/funnel]?"
```

### Number/Data Hooks

Specificity equals credibility.

```
"$[specific number] in [timeframe]. No [common requirement]. Here's the breakdown."
"I tested [number] approaches. Only [small number] worked. Here they are."
"[Percentage]% of [audience] don't know this about [topic]."
"It takes exactly [number] [days/steps/minutes] to [achieve result]."
"[Number] words. That's all it took to turn a $0 product into a $[amount] product."
"I analyzed [number] successful [things]. They all share [number] patterns."
```

### Story-Opening Hooks

Transport the reader into a narrative.

```
"I'll never forget the day [dramatic moment]."
"[Time period] ago, I was [relatable bad situation]. Today, [transformation]."
"My [family member/friend] said [discouraging quote]. I proved them wrong."
"The email came at [specific time]. I almost didn't open it."
"I was sitting in [specific place] when I realized [insight]."
"Nobody told me [truth about topic]. I had to learn it the hard way."
```

## Hook Power Words

Words that trigger psychological responses:

### Urgency Words
- Now, Today, Immediately, Before, Deadline, Limited, Last chance, Running out, Closing, Final

### Curiosity Words
- Secret, Hidden, Underground, Little-known, Insider, Revealed, Discovered, Untold, Decoded, Exposed

### Exclusivity Words
- Private, Invitation-only, Members-only, Elite, Exclusive, Behind-the-scenes, Inner circle, VIP, Select, Handpicked

### Credibility Words
- Proven, Tested, Research-backed, Guaranteed, Verified, Documented, Case study, Data-driven, Evidence-based, Peer-reviewed

### Emotional Trigger Words
- Finally, Imagine, Transform, Breakthrough, Effortless, Freedom, Escape, Unlock, Eliminate, Conquer

### Authority Words
- Expert, Authority, Pioneer, Founder, Creator, Award-winning, Recognized, Trusted, Leading, Certified

## The Content Grid Method

For generating unlimited hook variations, cross-reference:

**Y-Axis (Objections)**:
- "It's too expensive"
- "I don't have time"
- "I'm not sure it works"
- "I've tried everything"
- "It's not for me"

**X-Axis (Story Types)**:
- Origin Story
- Ability Story
- Morality Story
- Plan B Story
- This vs. That
- Regret Story
- Testimonial Story

**Each cell = one unique hook** that addresses a specific objection through a specific story angle. A 5x7 grid = 35 unique hooks from one product.

Example cell (Objection: "Too expensive" + Story: Origin):
> "I spent $50K on marketing courses before I found the $27 guide that actually worked."

Example cell (Objection: "Not sure it works" + Story: Testimonial):
> "Sarah was skeptical too. Then she made $8,200 in her first 67 days."

## Platform-Specific Hook Rules

### Facebook
- First line must work BEFORE the "See more" fold (~125 characters)
- Use line breaks for visual breathing room
- Emojis are acceptable but use sparingly (1-2 max in hook)
- Questions perform well as opening hooks

### Instagram
- First line is everything — same fold rules as Facebook
- Visual hook (image/video first frame) must match text hook
- Hashtags go at the end, never in the hook

### X (Twitter)
- Entire hook must fit in 280 characters
- Thread openers: use the "1/ " format with a strong standalone hook
- Avoid links in the hook tweet (kills reach)

### Google Ads
- Headline 1: Core hook (30 chars max)
- Headline 2: Supporting hook or qualifier
- Headline 3: CTA or urgency
- Description: Expand the hook with benefits

### Email
- Subject line IS the hook (50 chars ideal, 70 max)
- Preview text extends the hook (90 chars)
- First sentence of body must continue the hook's momentum
- Curiosity and pain-point hooks outperform all others in email

### YouTube
- Hook must land in first 5 seconds (before skip button)
- Open with the most surprising or emotional element
- "What you're about to see" or "Watch what happens when" work well
- Pattern interrupts (unexpected visuals) boost hook retention

### TikTok
- Text hook must appear on screen in first 1-2 seconds
- Spoken hook must start immediately (no intros)
- "POV:", "Wait for it:", and "The [adjective] part:" are native TikTok hook formats
- Controversy and contrarian hooks perform best on TikTok
