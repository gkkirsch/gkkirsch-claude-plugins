# Ad Copy Frameworks — Direct Response Copywriting Reference

This document contains proven, industry-standard advertising frameworks used by top direct response copywriters. Each framework includes structure, psychology, when to use it, and platform-specific guidance.

---

## 1. AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

The foundational framework for all persuasive writing. Every piece of copy follows this progression whether explicitly or implicitly.

### Structure

1. **Attention**: Stop the scroll. Use a bold claim, surprising statistic, provocative question, or pattern interrupt. You have 2.6 seconds.
2. **Interest**: Build engagement by addressing a relevant pain point, telling a relatable story, or presenting a compelling "what if." Make them think "this is about me."
3. **Desire**: Create emotional connection to the solution. Paint the after-state vividly. Use social proof, specific results, and sensory language. Make them feel the transformation.
4. **Action**: Clear, specific, urgent CTA. Remove friction. Tell them exactly what to do and what happens next.

### Psychology

- Follows the brain's natural decision-making sequence: notice → evaluate → want → act
- Each stage must be satisfied before the next can work
- Skipping stages (e.g., jumping to CTA without building desire) kills conversion

### When to Use

- Long-form copy (email sequences, sales pages, video scripts)
- Google Ads (headline = attention, description = interest/desire, CTA = action)
- Any ad over 100 words

### Example (Facebook Ad)

```
[ATTENTION] "I made $0 my first 6 months online. Then I changed ONE thing."

[INTEREST] Like most people, I was doing everything the gurus said — posting daily, running ads, building funnels. Nothing worked. I was burning cash and losing hope.

[DESIRE] Then I discovered the Extraction Method. Within 30 days, I had my first $5K month. Within 90 days, I hit $23K. Not because I worked harder — because I finally understood what actually makes people buy.

[ACTION] I put the entire system into a step-by-step guide. Grab it for $27 before the price doubles Friday. Link in comments.
```

## 2. PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

The most powerful short-form framework. Works because it exploits loss aversion — people feel losses 2.5x more intensely than equivalent gains (Kahneman & Tversky).

### Structure

1. **Problem**: Name the specific problem your audience faces. Be precise. Use their exact language. Show you understand their situation at a granular level.
2. **Agitate**: Twist the knife. Amplify the pain. Show the consequences of inaction. Make the status quo intolerable. Don't be cruel — be honest about what happens if nothing changes.
3. **Solution**: Present your offer as the bridge from pain to relief. Transition should feel like exhaling after holding your breath.

### Psychology

- Activates the brain's threat detection system (amygdala)
- Agitation creates cognitive dissonance between current state and desired state
- Solution provides relief, which creates positive association with your offer
- Works because people are more motivated to escape pain than pursue pleasure

### When to Use

- Short-form social media ads (Facebook, Instagram, X)
- Email subject lines and openers
- Ad headlines and hooks
- Any copy under 200 words

### Platform-Specific Notes

- **Facebook**: Problem in first line (before "See more"), agitate in body, solution + CTA at end
- **Google Ads**: Problem in headline, agitate in description line 1, solution in description line 2
- **Email**: Problem in subject line, agitate in opening, solution in body

### Example (Instagram Ad)

```
[PROBLEM] Spending 4+ hours/day creating content that gets 12 likes and zero sales?

[AGITATE] Meanwhile, your competitors are posting half as much and making 10x the revenue. Every day you waste on content that doesn't convert is another day your business bleeds money. At this rate, you'll burn out before you break even.

[SOLUTION] The Content-to-Cash System turns every post into a sales asset. 47 plug-and-play templates. No guesswork. Your first converting post goes live tonight. Tap the link.
```

## 3. BAB — Before, After, Bridge

The transformation framework. Paints a vivid contrast between current reality and desired future, then positions your product as the bridge.

### Structure

1. **Before**: Describe the prospect's current painful reality. Use specific, relatable details. Mirror their internal dialogue. Make them feel seen.
2. **After**: Paint the desired future state in vivid sensory detail. What does their life look like, feel like, sound like after the transformation? Be specific — "financial freedom" is weak; "checking your phone at 10am to see $2,300 in overnight sales while your kids eat pancakes" is strong.
3. **Bridge**: Your product/service is the bridge between Before and After. Explain the mechanism briefly. Show it's achievable.

### Psychology

- Exploits contrast bias: the greater the gap between Before and After, the more valuable the Bridge appears
- The After state activates the brain's reward system (dopamine)
- When people vividly imagine a better future, they psychologically commit to it
- The Bridge feels like a logical conclusion, not a pitch

### When to Use

- Testimonial-style ads
- Case study content
- Before/after transformation posts
- Email marketing
- Video ad scripts (especially for the first 30 seconds)

### Example (Email)

```
Subject: My Tuesday used to look like this...

[BEFORE]
6am alarm. Commute. 9 hours at a desk doing work I didn't care about. Commute home. Too tired to do anything meaningful. Repeat 260 times a year.

[AFTER]
Now? I wake up when I'm done sleeping. I open my laptop to see what sold overnight (usually $800-$2,000). I spend 2 hours on my business, then I'm done. The rest of the day is mine.

[BRIDGE]
The difference wasn't talent or luck. It was learning how to build a digital product that sells while I sleep. I documented the entire process — from zero to $15K/month — in The Extraction Blueprint.

If your Tuesdays still look like my old ones, this is how you change that. [Link]
```

## 4. The 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

A comprehensive framework that builds belief systematically. Particularly effective for skeptical audiences who've seen it all.

### Structure

1. **Promise**: Make a specific, compelling, believable promise. Not vague aspirations — concrete outcomes with timeframes. "Lose 15 pounds in 60 days" not "get healthier."
2. **Picture**: Help them visualize life after the promise is fulfilled. Use future pacing: "Imagine opening your laptop Monday morning to see..." Engage multiple senses.
3. **Proof**: Back up the promise with evidence. Testimonials, data, case studies, credentials, demonstrations. Stack multiple types of proof for maximum credibility.
4. **Push**: Create urgency and give a clear directive. Combine a reason to act now (scarcity, deadline, bonus) with an explicit CTA. Remove risk with a guarantee.

### Psychology

- Promise activates desire (dopamine)
- Picture creates mental ownership (endowment effect — they start to feel like it's already theirs)
- Proof satisfies the logical brain that wants to justify the emotional decision
- Push overcomes inertia by making "now" more attractive than "later"

### When to Use

- Product launch campaigns
- Webinar/event promotion
- High-ticket offers where belief is the main barrier
- Retargeting ads (they've seen the promise, now stack proof)
- Long-form Facebook ads

### Example (Facebook Ad — Long Form)

```
[PROMISE]
Build a $10K/month digital product business in 90 days — even if you have zero audience, zero tech skills, and zero product ideas right now.

[PICTURE]
Imagine 90 days from now: You wake up, check your phone, and see 14 new orders came in overnight. $847 while you slept. Your Stripe dashboard shows a clean uptrend — $3K last month, $7K this month, trending toward $12K next month. You finally close the tab on those job listings.

[PROOF]
This isn't hypothetical. 2,847 students have gone through The Product Extraction Method:
- Sarah K. went from corporate burnout to $8,200/month in 67 days
- Marcus T. built his first product in a weekend and hit $4,100 in month one
- Jennifer R. replaced her $95K salary in 4 months (then doubled it)
The method has generated over $12M in student revenue. It works because it's built on direct response principles, not "build it and they will come" fantasies.

[PUSH]
The program is $297 for the next 48 hours (regular price: $497). Includes a 60-day money-back guarantee — if you don't make at least $1,000, you pay nothing.

Grab your spot before the timer hits zero: [Link]
```

## 5. Star-Story-Solution

The narrative framework. Uses storytelling to bypass the prospect's natural sales resistance. People lower their guard for stories.

### Structure

1. **Star**: Introduce a relatable character (you, a customer, or a composite). Make them someone your audience identifies with. Establish their starting point — it should mirror your prospect's current situation.
2. **Story**: Tell their journey. Include the struggle, the failed attempts, the frustration, the turning point, and the discovery. Follow the emotional arc: low point → catalyst → breakthrough. Keep it specific with dates, numbers, and sensory details.
3. **Solution**: Reveal how the star solved their problem (your product/service). Show the results. Connect it back to the reader: "If [Star] can do it, so can you."

### Psychology

- Stories activate mirror neurons — listeners literally experience the protagonist's journey
- Narrative transportation bypasses analytical resistance (people don't argue with stories)
- The "identification effect": when readers see themselves in the Star, the Star's solution becomes their solution
- Specific details increase believability exponentially

### When to Use

- Video ad scripts (especially Facebook/Instagram/YouTube)
- Email sequences
- Case study ads
- Any time your audience is skeptical or has "seen it all"
- When you have strong customer success stories

### 13 Story Types (Proven Ad Story Archetypes)

1. **Origin Story**: Your lowest point → what changed → discovery → results
2. **Ability Story**: Impressive result achieved despite severe limitations
3. **Morality Story**: Choosing integrity over easy profit — and winning bigger
4. **Plan B Story**: Failure that led to a better approach
5. **Historical Story**: Past event parallels current opportunity
6. **Meet the Guru Story**: Wisdom from a mentor that changed everything
7. **Regret Story**: Missed opportunity → urgency for current opportunity
8. **Adventure Story**: Risk taken → reward that exceeded expectations
9. **This vs. That Story**: Old approach vs. new approach comparison
10. **Victory Story**: Overcoming a significant challenge with specific strategy
11. **Rewards Story**: Achievement and the journey that led to it
12. **Testimonial Story**: Someone else's success with your product
13. **Charity/Giving Story**: Purpose and profit working together

Each story type follows the same skeleton:
- **HOOK**: Pattern-interrupting opening line
- **BACKGROUND**: Context and setup
- **CONFLICT**: The problem or challenge
- **RESOLUTION**: The breakthrough and results
- **CTA**: What to do next

## 6. Triple Hook (Premise-Stakes-Twist)

The opening framework for video scripts and scroll-stopping content. Designed to capture attention in the first 3-5 seconds.

### Structure

1. **Premise**: A bold, attention-grabbing statement that establishes credibility or makes a compelling claim. Must be specific and impressive. "I generate $7,000/day from my laptop."
2. **Stakes**: Why this matters. What's at risk if they don't pay attention. Create urgency and importance. "And if you're still trading time for money, you're running out of runway."
3. **Twist**: An unexpected pattern interrupt that challenges conventional wisdom and creates curiosity. Makes people stop and think "wait, what?" "The crazy part? I don't have a website, an email list, or any social media following."

### Psychology

- Premise hooks attention through specificity and boldness
- Stakes create emotional investment through loss aversion
- Twist creates a curiosity gap that demands resolution
- The three elements together create an irresistible cognitive pull

### The PSTBCR Extension (Full Video Script)

For video scripts beyond the hook:
1. **P**remise → **S**takes → **T**wist (first 15 seconds)
2. **B**ackground: Context that builds your case (15-30 seconds)
3. **C**onflict: The problem, why traditional approaches fail (30-45 seconds)
4. **R**esolution: Your solution and specific results (45-60 seconds)

### When to Use

- Opening of ANY video ad (Facebook, Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube)
- First 3 lines of a text ad (before the "See more" fold)
- Email subject line + first sentence
- Podcast ad openings

### Triple Hook Examples

```
"Most people won't believe what I'm about to tell you, [PREMISE]
but my business generates over $7,000 a day, [STAKES]
and virtually no one knows who I am." [TWIST]

"I spent $50,000 on marketing courses, [PREMISE]
and every single one of them was wrong about one thing, [STAKES]
the thing that actually makes people buy." [TWIST]

"A single mom with no degree and $200 to her name [PREMISE]
just built a $30K/month business [STAKES]
using a method that most marketing experts say doesn't work." [TWIST]
```

## Framework Selection Guide

| Scenario | Best Framework | Why |
|----------|---------------|-----|
| Short social ad (< 150 words) | PAS | Fastest emotional impact |
| Long-form Facebook ad | 4Ps or AIDA | Builds belief systematically |
| Video script | Triple Hook + Star-Story-Solution | Captures attention, tells a story |
| Email sequence | BAB or Star-Story-Solution | Paints transformation vividly |
| Google Search ad | PAS (compressed) | Limited characters, max impact |
| Retargeting ad | 4Ps (emphasize Proof + Push) | They know you; now convince them |
| Testimonial/case study | Star-Story-Solution | Real stories sell |
| Product launch | AIDA + 4Ps hybrid | Comprehensive persuasion |
| Webinar/event promo | BAB | Future pacing drives registrations |
| Cold audience first touch | Triple Hook + PAS | Stop scroll, hit pain, offer relief |

## The 6 Cognitive Biases for Retargeting

When writing retargeting ads (prospects who've already seen your offer), leverage these biases:

1. **Social Proof**: Show others succeeding. Testimonials, numbers, crowd validation.
2. **Power of Now / Urgency**: Opportunity cost of waiting. Real consequences of delay. Not fake scarcity — genuine urgency.
3. **Authority**: Establish expertise. Credentials, unique insights, industry corrections.
4. **Power of Free**: Highlight bonuses and added value. What they get that they didn't expect.
5. **Category Heuristics**: Break down exactly what's included. Make the offer tangible and concrete.
6. **Scarcity**: Legitimate limited availability. What changes after the deadline. What they miss.

Each retargeting ad should:
- Open with a scroll-stopping Triple Hook
- Address the specific bias angle
- Handle the #1 objection for that audience segment
- Include image/video recommendations
- End with a clear, low-friction CTA
